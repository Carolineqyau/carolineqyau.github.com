Overview

Problem

A lot of products are planned to break so that companies can sell more to the consumers. This creates a lot of waste and gives a financial burden to the consumers on overbuying. According to latest projections, the global population could grow to around 8.5 billion in 2030, 9.7 billion in 2050. The equivalent of almost three planets is required to provide the natural resources we need to sustain our lifestyles.

Responsible Consumption and Production is the 12th United Nation Sustainable Development Goals by 2030, to promote prosperity while protecting the planet. 93 per cent of the world’s 250 largest companies are now reporting on sustainability.

If we could encourage consumers to purchase products that have good quality and are sustainable, there would be much less waste and the products would cost less in the long run because it lasts.

How can we decrease irresponsible production and consumption?

Brainstorm

We first brainstormed ideas on how irresponsible production and consumption looks like, then categorized the problems into different groups. We further discussed how responsible production and consumption looks like.

Problem Statement

We decided to focus on the problem sector — Planned Obsolescence, which is a policy of producing consumer goods that rapidly become obsolete and so require replacing, achieved by frequent changes in design, termination of the supply of spare parts, and the use of nondurable materials. Examples include fast fashion, smartphones, ink cartridges and so on.
We pinpointed the cause and effect of planned obsolescence, as well as the stakeholders that face this problem — consumers and the environment, and formulated the problem statement:
Due to a profit driven economy, consumers are accustomed to wasteful consumption which results in an emphasis on materialism leading to pollution, landfill, and lesser quality of life.

How to tackle the problem from the business side?

It’s in businesses’ interest to find new solutions that enable sustainable consumption and production patterns. A better understanding of environmental and social impacts of products and services is needed, both of product life cycles and how these are affected by use within lifestyles. Identifying “hot spots” within the value chain where interventions have the greatest potential to improve the environmental and social impact of the system as a whole is a crucial first step. Innovation and design solutions can both enable and inspire individuals to lead more sustainable lifestyles, reducing impacts and improving well-being.

How can a consumer help?

1. Reducing their waste
2. Being thoughtful about what they buy and choosing a sustainable option whenever possible.
Making informed purchases also helps. For example, the textile industry today is the second largest polluter of clean water after agriculture, and many fashion companies exploit textile workers in the developing world. If a consumer can buy from sustainable and local sources, they can make a difference as well as exercising pressure on businesses to adopt sustainable practices.

Target Audience

Our target audience is millennials who live in urban cities and want to be environmental conscious but don’t know how to find sustainable products. Millennials in the United States spend about $600 billion a year, so that’s a lot of spending power.
